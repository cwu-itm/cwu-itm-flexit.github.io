Introduction

We need to be able to think holistically in order to abandon linear, fragmented thinking in presuming that the system is defined by the interconnections among components and its end goal. With this view, we can divide the world into the Five System Types proposed by Checkland namely Natural, Designed Physical, Designed Abstract, Human Activity, and Transcendental which can be successfully handled by the systems engineering technique.

Definitions

System

A system is a group of things that are working together as parts of a mechanism, interconnected network, principles, and procedures. In tech this goes a step further by allowing to accept input, process data, produce output, while also storing the data.

System Thinking

System thinking is the process of thinking of systems rather than a isolated part. That means considering a problem on a larger scale helping source a solution as a whole system rather than attempting to isolate the problem. This creates long-sustained success rather than small snapshots of success. Creating a understanding of a full image and always making sure that you start bottom up to determine the best solution for the entire system rather than specific sections.

Systems Engineering

Is the encompassing approach of designing, integrating, and managing complex systems over their lifecycles, guaranteeing stakeholder needs are satisfied based on the full scope of the work completed.

Shift to Holistic Thinking

In modern world technical environment, taking a project as a mere list of tasks is a formula to failure. We need to embrace Systems Thinking, a thinking process, which ceases to be linear but more holistic. System is not only the things that are included in it, but it is characterized by the Interconnections of elements in it, its Boundary and the purpose that it is finally intended to fulfill.

Explaining the Different Systems

Being able to breakdown how each of these systems play a larger part in how we create/ design change in the future in this digital environment, being organized and understanding how each play part is key to future literacy.

Using Checkalnd's Five System Types to categorize we discover these System types:

Natural Systems:

    • Natural systems are an open system with features and behaviors not controlled by humans or their actions. As they form spontaneously, without any human design, these systems tend to respond to the outside world on their own, without the human intervention. Examples include the human body, weather patterns, ecosystems, biological functions, and so on. However, a grasp of natural systems is important in the context of modern systems engineering as they often represent the environment in which engineered solutions have to function, with variables that are unpredictable and impose strict limitations on the overall system architecture or functionality.

Designed Physical Systems:

    • Designed physical systems are the physical real world expression of Human Design. These systems are made from physical components carefully designed to provide humans or automated agents with a very specific function to carry out. The examples of these systems are numerous, ranging from simple hand tools, such as hammers, to complex systems, such as cars, aircraft, computers and medical appliances. Our present digital world is built on designed physical systems that are embedded with electronics and are connected to physical interfaces, which enable the computational logic to engage with the material world.

Designed Abstract Systems:

    • Abstract systems are systems designed by a person with no physical existence, but constructed deliberately to assist the human mind to remain in structured and organized systems of thought. These systems have no physical correspondences and depend solely on a symbolic representation to explain or organize complex ideas and concepts. These range from philosophical ideas, mathematical theories, formal languages, and engineering models, among others. In a digital world, designed abstract systems are the backbone of digital information processing, organization and understanding, and they are realized as software algorithms, protocol architectures, data structures, and logic frameworks.

Human Activity Systems:

    • Human activity systems are intentional, individual humans-oriented systems that are deliberately designed by people to accomplish a shared goal or specific set of collective goals. There are certain characteristics of these systems which make them highly dynamic as they directly influence, demonstrate and control human behavior and interaction in the real world. Examples in everyday life include organizational hierarchy, politics, social groups and services to people. It is crucial in systems engineering, because the implementation of any digital transformation depends largely on the ability to match the behavioral processes of the people who are using the software tools.

Transcendental Systems:

    • Systems transcendental are the systems of absolute truth, metaphysics, divinity, and conscious nature. They work at the extreme range of human perception, and include unknown problems and complex universal properties which are not currently understood, let alone measurable, by science. They are not particularly relevant to systems engineering, but they provide an important edge to the future literacy and will bring designers back to the absolute limit of what is technologically possible today, and provide a touch of theoretical space that future discovery and innovation in the field will take place.

Complexity (necessary and accidental), breakdown in Communication, lack of Understanding, the evil traps that we have faced in complex projects, we will need a strict method on how to overcome these. Systems Engineering provides the common sense, multi-disciplinary approach necessary to overcome these challenges to deliver successful systems.

MBSE: The One Vocational Truth.

From the literature provided, it seems that Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) is a paradigm shift from the disconnected stack of papers, which is prone to maintenance errors, different interpretation from their human owners and has a significant lack of verifiability. Historically, projects have become so large that devoting all your time to reading and updating the same stack of paper folders wasn't always possible, and if a change was made in one dimension, you had to make it in other dimensions, which could mean that one was the wrong dimension, and you had no way of knowing. To address these weaknesses, MBSE is centered around intelligent digital models that can be linked together with requirements, architectures and interfaces all interwoven.

This modern methodology is centered on the digital modeling, the development of a digital model, which can be a Systems Architect Model (SAM) or textual system representations based on a language such as SysML V2, which is an authoritative Single Source of Truth. To enable early analysis, strict traceability and ongoing validation, as noted by Khushnood Adil Rafique et al., these central artifacts are shared amongst all stakeholders. MBSE not only brings this computation environment together in a centralized place, it also ensures that updates automatically flow through to ensure consistency within the system, enabling a multidisciplinary team to collaborate on the same reliable information throughout a product's life cycle.
